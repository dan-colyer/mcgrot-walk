Audio Profile: Ishbel Fothergill is a self-taught street phrenologist who reads skulls for a tin of beans, voice prim and clinical even as she squints at a half-torn page.
Scene: A card table under a collapsed bus shelter, a chipped plaster head as her only prop, rain dripping through the broken roof.
Director's Notes: Style — clipped, lecturing, like a Victorian instructional filmstrip, faltering with genuine irritation whenever the torn page cuts her off. Accent — Morningside-prim, every consonant crisply enunciated. Pacing — brisk and didactic, with an abrupt stumble at each tear. CRITICAL: the quoted comic text contains misspellings and nonsense words; read every quoted line EXACTLY as printed, pronouncing nonsense phonetically with complete confidence. Never correct or smooth them.
