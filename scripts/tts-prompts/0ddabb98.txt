Audio Profile: Josie Beattie is a sharp-tongued dockside pamphleteer who reads the news aloud to anyone standing still long enough, voice indignant and quick.
Scene: A market stall wallpapered with torn newspaper clippings, cauldron bubbling nearby, gulls squabbling overhead. She performs the whole thing like a furious editorial, jabbing a spoon for punctuation.
Director's Notes: Style — indignant, fast editorial delivery, building outrage. Accent — sharp Leith docker patter, clipped consonants. Pacing — quick and jabbing, slowing only for the final toast. CRITICAL: the quoted comic text contains misspellings and nonsense words; read every quoted line EXACTLY as printed, pronouncing nonsense phonetically with complete confidence. Never correct or smooth them.

Josie: [indignant] McGrot! The Kyiv Conundrum. [reading a clipping aloud, mocking] "Putin team trolls its way to Ukraine summit with Trump as serves' Chicken Kyiv for in-flight meal." [scoffing] "Chicken Kyiv? They've nae clue aboot marrow magic..." [scribbling furiously] "The meat's getting ideas again..." [reading her own notes] "Dockside Kyiv — stuffed wi dockweed, marrow sauce, an Leith grit." [stirring, a bird squawks and flees] [proud, rising] "Global politics may flap its gums, but only McGrot feeds Leith true diplomacy." [raising a plate, then cutting off mid-sentence as if the page itself ran out] "Aye — diplomacy that tastes like—" [trails off, comic ends there]
