Audio Profile: Jinty Baxter is a weather-cracked loch boatman who bottles suspicious lochwater and hawks it as tonic, voice like a foghorn wrapped in wet wool.
Scene: A flooded jetty on a grey loch shore, water lapping, an occasional distant honk from something large and unseen out on the water. He performs the comic to a dripping dog at his feet, entirely unbothered by the damp.
Director's Notes: Style — hushed conspiratorial fisherman's yarn that swells into proud bellowing. Accent — briny West Highland boatman's burr, rolling and unhurried, sudden emphasis on key words. Pacing — slow drift that quickens sharply for shouted lines. CRITICAL: the quoted comic text contains misspellings and nonsense words; read every quoted line EXACTLY as printed, pronouncing nonsense phonetically with complete confidence. Never correct or smooth them.

Jinty: [clears throat, briny] McGrot! Milk of the Loch. [rowing mime, wistful] ...out on the water wi' the dug, and her. [reverent, sniffing an invisible jar] "The milk smelt like war medals and deep water secrets." [holding it aloft] Essence of Myth. [reading a sign, uneasy] The Gullet. "Nessie Mukshake — only screans once." [alarmed muttering, several voices] [tasting, wide-eyed] "I can taste the Battle of Culloden... and my grandmother?" [grave nod] "We knew this day would come." [chest out, defiant, green-mouthed] "Tell yer parliament. Nessie chose ME." [long satisfied exhale] ...Magic, that.
