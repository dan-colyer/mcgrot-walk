Façade photographs (17)

Real Leith Walk buildings, via Wikimedia Commons — many originally from geograph.org.uk. Reused under their Creative Commons licences; each is credited to its photographer below.

The wall textures in the walk are derivative works of these photographs: each was perspective-corrected to a flat elevation, cut into ground-floor and upper-storey bands, and colour-graded. Where the source is licensed CC BY-SA, that share-alike obligation carries over to the derived texture, and attribution is retained here.

6 of these tiles — the blander, already-shuttered or derelict units — were further aged with an AI image-editing model (FLUX.1 Kontext): rust, boarding, graffiti and grime added over the real geometry, which is preserved. These remain derivative works of the source photographs and keep their original attribution and licence.
